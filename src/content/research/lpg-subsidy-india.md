preparedFor: "The World Bank"
summary: "India's Pahal program was, at launch, the world's largest direct cash transfer scheme. How much did it actually save? The Government of India, the CAG, and the IISD each arrived at dramatically different answers - and the gap between them runs into hundreds of billions of rupees. This study examines why, and what it means for the design of large-scale subsidy programs."
